### 4. High-Value Request Escalation

Not all requests are equal. "Book me a cab" vs. "I need a helicopter to Abu Dhabi in 2 hours" require different response urgency.

**Manual approach:**
All messages mixed together. You're responding in order received. The urgent helicopter request is buried under 10 routine messages.

**Smart triage system:**

Requests tagged by:
- **Urgency:** Immediate (within 4 hours), Today, This week, Future
- **Value:** Ultra-high ($5,000+), High ($1,000-5,000), Standard ($500-1,000), Routine ($0-500)
- **Complexity:** Simple (single vendor), Medium (coordination required), Complex (multiple moving parts)

**Automatic prioritization:**

Your dashboard sorts by priority score:
- Urgent + Ultra-high value + Complex = Handle NOW
- Today + High value = Handle within 2 hours
- This week + Standard = Handle within 8 hours
- Future + Routine = Batch process daily

**Example scenario:**

10 AM messages:
1. "Can you get me a table at Nobu sometime next week?" (Future, Standard, Simple)
2. "I need private jet to London tonight, 8 PM departure" (Immediate, Ultra-high, Complex)
3. "Car service to airport tomorrow at 9 AM" (Today, Routine, Simple)

**Your system flags:**
- Request #2 in flashing red: URGENT
- Request #3 in yellow: Handle today
- Request #1 in blue: Batch with other future requests

You immediately call your jet charter contact for #2. While waiting for callback, you quickly confirm #3. You batch-process #1 later with other restaurant requests.

**Impact:**

**Client satisfaction:** High-value urgent requests never buried
**Revenue protection:** You never miss the $15,000 jet charter because you were responding to $200 car service requests first
**Stress reduction:** Clear priority system prevents decision paralysis

**December scenario:**

Without triage: You handle requests in order, miss high-value urgent ones, lose $40,000 in jet charters and yacht bookings.

With triage: You capture every high-value urgent request, revenue protected.
