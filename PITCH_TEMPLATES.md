# Cold Outreach Pitch Templates

## Usage Guide

### When to Use Each Pitch Type

**LinkedIn DM Pitch (150-200 words)**
- First contact with prospects you've researched on LinkedIn
- Warm-ish outreach after engaging with their content
- Professional but conversational tone
- Goal: Start a conversation, not close a sale

**Email Pitch (250-350 words)**
- Colder outreach from targeted lists
- Follow-up after initial LinkedIn connection
- More detailed, includes social proof
- Goal: Book a discovery call

**WhatsApp Pitch (100-150 words)**
- Dubai/Middle East markets where WhatsApp dominates
- Follow-up after initial contact
- Ultra-concise, mobile-optimized
- Goal: Quick response, immediate conversation

### Personalization Guidelines

**Required Placeholders:**
- `[FIRST_NAME]` - Use their actual first name
- `[COMPANY_NAME]` - Their business name
- `[BLOG_URL]` - Link to the relevant blog post
- `[YOUR_NAME]` - Your name (Israel Chukwudi)
- `[CALENDAR_LINK]` - Your cal.com booking link

**Optional Personalization:**
- Reference their specific location (Dubai Marina, Courchevel, specific neighborhood)
- Mention a recent post/achievement if you've done research
- Adjust dollar/currency amounts based on their market (AED for Dubai, EUR for Alps)
- Use their industry-specific language

### Sending Best Practices

1. **Never mass-send without personalization** - Change at least 30% of the template per prospect
2. **Send at optimal times:**
   - LinkedIn DMs: Tuesday-Thursday, 9-11 AM their timezone
   - Emails: Tuesday/Wednesday mornings
   - WhatsApp: Avoid early morning (before 9 AM) or late evening (after 8 PM)
3. **Follow up once** - If no response in 5-7 days, send one follow-up, then move on
4. **Track what works** - Note which variations get responses, iterate accordingly

## Final Notes on Personalization

### Before Sending ANY Pitch:

1. **Research the prospect** - 5 minutes on their website/LinkedIn
2. **Find specific pain point** - Which scenario from the blog matches their situation?
3. **Customize opening** - Reference something specific about their business
4. **Adjust numbers** - Use their market's currency and realistic figures
5. **Check timing** - Are they currently in peak season or planning mode?

### Follow-Up Strategy (If No Response):

**Wait 5-7 days, then send ONE follow-up:**

**Short Follow-Up Template:**
"Hi [FIRST_NAME], following up on my message about [SPECIFIC PAIN POINT].

Totally understand if timing isn't right, but wanted to make sure you saw it since [SPECIFIC STAT FROM PITCH] is a pretty significant opportunity cost.

If you're interested in seeing how [SOLUTION] works, I'm happy to show you in 15 minutes. If not, no worries at all.

Let me know.

- [YOUR_NAME]"

**If no response to follow-up:** Move on gracefully. Don't become spam.

### Success Metrics to Track:

- **Response rate** - Target: 15-25% for LinkedIn, 10-15% for cold email
- **Meeting booking rate** - Target: 40-60% of responses
- **Pitch variations** - Test different hooks, see what resonates
- **Time to response** - Faster = warmer lead

### Remember:

The goal is to start a conversation, not close a sale in the DM. Be helpful, consultative, and genuinely interested in solving their problem. If they're not a fit, that's fine. Move on to the next prospect.

These templates work because they lead with empathy, speak to real pain points with specific numbers, position you as the guide (not the hero), and make it easy to take the next step.

Now go start some conversations.
